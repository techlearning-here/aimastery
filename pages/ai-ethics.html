üö® Core Ethical Principles

1. Transparency & Disclosure

Always disclose when content is AI-generated, especially in:

  • Academic work and research
  • Professional publications
  • Client deliverables
  • Public communications
  • Creative works

Appropriate Disclosure

  • "This article was written with AI assistance and edited by [human]"
  • "Image generated with Midjourney and refined manually"
  • "Research compiled using Perplexity AI, verified by [human]"

Don't Do This

  • Presenting AI-generated content as entirely human-created
  • Using AI to impersonate others
  • Hiding AI involvement in critical decisions

2. Verification & Accuracy

Never trust AI output blindly. Always:

Verification Checklist

  • Fact-Check: Verify statistics, quotes, and claims
  • Cross-Reference: Check multiple sources
  • Expert Review: Have domain experts validate technical content
  • Test Code: Run and test AI-generated code thoroughly
  • Legal Review: Have lawyers review legal AI outputs

AI Hallucinations

AI can confidently state false information. Always verify:

  • Historical facts and dates
  • Scientific claims
  • Legal precedents
  • Statistical data
  • Technical specifications
  • Citations and sources

3. Privacy & Data Protection

What NOT to Share with AI

  • Passwords or API keys
  • Personal Identifiable Information (PII)
  • Confidential business data
  • Patient medical records (HIPAA protected)
  • Financial account details
  • Trade secrets or proprietary information
  • Attorney-client privileged information

Best Practices

  • Anonymize data before input
  • Use placeholder names/values
  • Check company AI usage policies
  • Use privacy-focused AI tools when needed
  • Understand where your data is stored
  • Enable privacy modes when available

4. Bias & Fairness

AI models can perpetuate and amplify biases. Be aware:

Common Biases

  • Gender bias: Stereotypical career associations
  • Racial bias: Underrepresentation or stereotypes
  • Age bias: Assumptions about capabilities
  • Cultural bias: Western-centric perspectives
  • Socioeconomic bias: Privilege assumptions

How to Mitigate

  • Review outputs for bias and stereotypes
  • Use inclusive language in prompts
  • Test outputs from diverse perspectives
  • Manually edit to ensure fairness
  • Get diverse feedback on content
  • Challenge problematic outputs

5. Copyright & Intellectual Property

Key Rules:

  • AI-generated content may not be copyrightable (varies by jurisdiction)
  • AI trained on copyrighted material raises legal questions
  • Using AI doesn't exempt you from copyright law
  • Respect original creators and attribution

Best Practices

  • Understand terms of service for each AI tool
  • Don't use AI to recreate copyrighted works
  • Give credit when using AI for derivative works
  • Purchase appropriate licenses when needed
  • Consult legal counsel for commercial use
  • Respect "living artist" restrictions (e.g., Midjourney)

üìã AI Use Policy Checklist

Before using AI for any project, check:

Academic Context

  • Does my institution allow AI use for this assignment?
  • Have I cited AI assistance properly?
  • Is my work still primarily my own intellectual contribution?
  • Have I verified all facts and sources?
  • Would this be considered plagiarism?

Professional Context

  • Does my company have an AI use policy?
  • Am I allowed to use external AI tools with company data?
  • Should I disclose AI use to clients?
  • Have I verified accuracy for business decisions?
  • Am I maintaining data security standards?

Creative Context

  • Am I respecting copyright and attribution?
  • Is disclosure appropriate for my audience?
  • Have I added sufficient human creativity?
  • Am I avoiding prohibited content (violence, hate, etc.)?
  • Do I understand ownership rights?

Unethical uses of AI that you must avoid:

Using AI to generate fake reviews or testimonials

Using AI for surveillance without consent
Generating misleading information intentionally
Replacing human judgment in critical decisions
Using AI for illegal activities

üìä Responsible AI Workflow

Before Using AI

  1. Check policies: Company, school, client agreements
  2. Consider alternatives: Is AI necessary?
  3. Identify risks: Privacy, bias, accuracy concerns
  4. Plan oversight: How will you verify quality?

During Use

  1. Anonymize data: Remove sensitive information
  2. Use good prompts: Clear, unbiased instructions
  3. Review outputs: Check for bias, errors, appropriateness
  4. Document process: Track AI usage

After Use

  1. Verify accuracy: Fact-check all claims
  2. Edit for bias: Remove stereotypes and unfairness
  3. Add disclosure: Note AI assistance
  4. Get feedback: Have humans review
  5. Measure impact: Track outcomes and issues

üéØ Decision Framework: Should I Use AI?

Ask yourself these questions:

1. Is it legal and allowed?

  • Company policy permits
  • No contractual violations
  • Compliant with regulations
  • No prohibited content

2. Is it ethical?

  • Transparent and disclosed
  • Not harming others
  • Respecting privacy
  • Avoiding bias

3. Is it effective?

  • AI is right tool for task
  • Output quality acceptable
  • Time savings worthwhile
  • Human oversight feasible

4. Is it verifiable?

  • Can fact-check outputs
  • Can test functionality
  • Expert review available
  • Quality measurable
